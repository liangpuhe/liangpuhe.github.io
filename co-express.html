Individual Project
This project is an individual design project, aiming to solve a problem identified in the neighborhood. The project went through different design stages. The design techniques helped me organize my ideas, generate new ideas and constantly get feedback from my peers to refine the ideas. By seeing the techniques applied in various designs of my peers, I learnt that we also need to be flexible to adjust the approach for the best of individual project.
(Competitive analysis; Persona; Sketch; Brainstorm; Storyboard; Wireframe; QOC analysis; paper prototyping; high-fidelity prototyping.)

Define Problem

What The User Has to Say

My complain about the 31-room apartment (more than 60 tenants) I am living in, is that delivery men always stock everyone’s express packages behind the apartment door. Searching can take up to 5min each time.
1. Checking the name on the label is hard, as each express company uses different format.
2. One package is checked repetitively by different people.
3. Sometimes when one package is not picked immediately, it can be checked twice or more by a same person
I think an interactive device can save the searching effort by collaborating tenants who pick from the same spot.
5 people living in different apartments are interviewed. They reported the same issue, and some other shared needs in delivery picking.
1. People want to know when to start checking, as the delivery time reported online is always inaccurate.
2. Most interviewees mentioned they are willing to help roommates pick their packages if they see it.
3. When their hands are not available, instead of leaving all the packages for later, they choose to take as much as they can, and leave the rest for next time.

Competitive analysis

The Existing Design & The Missing Features

I compared existing settings of how people pick up deliveries.
People living in house have their own mailbox.
DHL box requires amount of space. usually one in a neighborhood. Take 5-10min walk from home.
Chinese deliverymen separate deliveries by marked names or phone numbers.

Prototype

Make It Real

In the prototyping phrase, I kept three things in mind, which I learnt from previous design stages and peer critiques:
1. Even though the features are brand new, I should draw designs from other Apps which users are familiar with, to make it intuitive to use.
2. Before users pick up their own package, to encourage them to do a favor for other tenants, the App should work without a hitch, and take very few time.
3. As color is used to identify packages, the App design itself should be simple, not taking up too much user cognition in colors.

The App allows user to choose the package owner, or by default send to all tenants living in the room print out a colored sticker with room number on it. Send SMS notification to the owner (and the roommates)

After picking the package, user can clean the status in the system

The App allows user to change message subscription, and add new tenants.

Healthy Food Tracker

2019 Google Design Exercise
Puhe Liang

Passion as a Designer

I love exercising and learning things. Myself believes food nutritions ensure my exercise and brain work in full performance.

I have two lovely cousins at 10 and 16. Their parents are too busy to take care of every meal. When I visited them, I often observe their food choice “unhealthy” to my standard, but failed to invite them for more nutritious options.

For this design challenge, I am very curious to understand kid’s nutrition problem in a deeper and broader level, design a food tracking experience that foster nutritious eating habit.

Tracking page

Understand the Problem

Nutrition is important for kid's growth. Fostering a healthy diet habit at a young age could benefit life health in a long run. However, maintaining a healthy diet is costive, and insufficient nutrition doesn't reflect on health problem shortly. When food access is limited due to time or dependency on parents, kids tend to give the priority to taste, which is usually fast food with high amount of fat and sodium.

I started with listing kids’ motivations and activities related to eating. It helps me understand the ambiguity in the problem domain, then come up with research questions.

Research questions

Research questions were categorized into kids, family and school. Some were answered by literature review in a quantitative scale. The others were further developed to interview questions for both parents and kids.

A screenshot of my notes on Bear

In interviews, I talked with three families whose kids are in pre-school, middle school or high school. This helps me pin down the problems and constraints, define the design space, and picture a day of my user in User Journey.

Design Space

With the gathered information, I decided the target user group and selected the key nutrition problems that I am going to tackle.

I picked to influence 13-18 year old teenagers as the major user, with following reasons:

1. Kids start to have nutrition/health courses around 7th grade. After learning basic nutritions, they start to care about their own health.

2. Kids older than 13 are usually less dependent to family or school’s meal arrangement. They tend to prepare their own meal or go to restaurant by themselves.

3. It is effective and lasting to foster healthy habit at a rather mature age range, before the kids start independent college lives.


I identified some important constraints that should apply to a nutrition tracker design:

1. Hard to measure various food source (school meal, snack, home-made meal, etc.).

2. Time and effort are reported as huge barrier for both parents and kids pursuing healthy diet.

3. Kids have shallow knowledge about nutrition and cooking, have difficulty in finding a good trade-off between convenience and nutrition.

4. Processed food is exposed frequently on media, creating a popular cool image.

Design Goals

A food tracking experience that fosters nutritious eating habit.


This will be achieved through following three design goals:

1. Easily track their nutrition intake, especially shortage

2. Understand the nutritions in food groups and their benefits or detriments

3. Feel proud of keeping a nutritious diet

User Journey


I created User Journey for a kid’s school day, identified time and context where kids possibly eat and log food.

Scanned hand drawing annotated in Sketch

Smart interface could engage kids more by including varieties and gamification. Timeliness and completeness are also important for food tracking. So I decided to use webpage together with mobile app to support the food tracking experience.

The advantage of mobile app is the use of camera, to take food pictures or scan barcode. In this design exercise, I will focus on the essential webpage design which is available for every kid. The webpage can potentially be part of a Chromebook App for education.

Competitive Analysis


I selected four competitors which target at different age groups. My goal is to study the features/gamification used to engage different user groups, and the pros/cons of the information they use for tracking.

Brainstorm & Sketch


With the design goals in mind, I sketched out feature ideas on papers. The sketched design is not all in good quality, but it helped me explore the potential ways of solving the problem. Meanwhile, it builds a collection of ideas that I could also look back in future iterations.

Wireframe


I use Sketch to draw wireframe, put together the idea pieces.


Firstly, I created layout of key features, then think about the navigation of the experience flow and interactivity of UI components. Lastly, to transit the wireframe to a hi-fi prototype, I refined the wireframe with selected font, size, and web column layout.

How to present food nutrition?

As found in interviews and competitive analysis, people care different aspects of food nutritions.

I think “food variety” fits into the design goal the most.

1. Having the portion of food in all five categories should be enough to provide needed nutritions, even though it is not the most accurate.

2. Food type is recognizable thus easy to track.

If the kids or parents want to look into the calories or underlying nutrition content, they still have the option to pull out a “technical” report.

How to present tracking Process?

The way of presentation should not only show the nutrition intake progress, but also inform the desired amount varies for each category. I explored many options to look for an effective visualization meanwhile attractive to kids.

After some iterations on feedback, I decided to use five separate graphs for each category, and use discrete dot array for tracking as it is easy to count and motivating kids.

How to engage User?

I want to take use of the social power and gamification to engage teenagers in tracking and feel proud of being healthy. Meanwhile, the design should be careful about reward or punishment mechanism, in case users log fake data to pursue reward and fame.

When design gamification, I have two goals:

1. Eat enough nutritious food in each category

2. Develop a long-lasting habit.

How to log Unique homemade dish?

One option is to log the ingredients. But I think logging a meal as a whole could better improve users' nutrition knowledge. Link back to the research finding, cooking skill is a barrier for teenagers to pursue healthy diet. When creating ingredient list of a meal, the user would also reflect the nutritions they took.

Reflection

1. One-time and long-term testing.

There are two types of testing I want to do. Firstly, I want to bring the hi-fi prototype to kids, see how they perceive the alternative visualizations and nutrition information. Secondly, the gamification design can be only verified overtime. The success can be measured by user engagement, such as the population, the amount of logging and the challenge accomplishment.

2. Design Goal. Design Goal. Design Goal.

When designing micro interaction in hi-fidelity prototype, there were some times I lost in maneuvering UI elements and forgot the overall design goals.  I couldn’t see things going wrong until I got a chance to look at the design as a whole. It makes prototyping unnecessarily long. I hope through practice, in the future, I could have a focus mind on design goals even when solving complex problem.

3. Other things can be done.

Nutrition tracking is a huge topic. There are other things I thought of but couldn’t include in this exercise.

• Barcode scan on Phone
• Allergy settings
• Track exercise. Exercise influences nutrition needs.
• Track water intake. Drinking water is a healthy habit to develop.
• Track food taking time. Change disordered eating habit.
